# Early Weapon Upgrades

## Design Goal

The first weapon choice in `Killer Queen` should not be:

- sword is cool, gun is boring
- one path is correct
- endgame power handed out immediately

It should be:

- pick your first weakness
- learn what this tool cannot do yet
- earn small fixes
- feel your kit sharpen in real time

These are `weak upgrades`, meaning:

- practical
- readable
- slightly empowering
- still far from the mid-game benchmark

## Shared Rules

- The starter weapon should work on minute one.
- The starter weapon should feel incomplete on minute five.
- The first upgrade should solve irritation, not finish the build.
- The second upgrade should unlock expression.
- The third upgrade should preview what the later real version becomes.
- The fourth upgrade should be the bridge into the mid-game benchmark, not the end of the tree.

## Arc Cutter Starter

Starter name:
- `Service Fang`

### Base State

Starting weaknesses:

- short reach
- two-hit ground string only
- weak airborne confirm
- no real armored pressure

What the player learns:

- closing distance is exciting
- missing is costly
- killing up close feels better than it is practical

### Upgrade 1: `Sharpened Edge`

Effect:
- slightly increases attack reach

Why it is good:
- immediate readability
- fixes early whiff frustration
- keeps the weapon weak but less cramped

What it does not do:
- no damage spike
- no crowd clear
- no combo complexity

### Upgrade 2: `Lift Notch`

Effect:
- adds a simple rising follow-up after the second ground hit or first air hit

Why it is good:
- starts teaching the stylish vertical identity
- makes Mite Drifters more fun without deleting risk

What it does not do:
- no infinite launcher
- no free anti-air dominance

### Upgrade 3: `Rebound Spring`

Effect:
- confirmed blade kills give a cleaner rebound and slightly better route carry

Why it is good:
- turns close-range success into visible movement payoff
- makes the weapon feel smarter without making it stronger at range

What it does not do:
- no giant damage increase
- no auto-break on everything

### Upgrade 4: `Dash Motor`

Effect:
- unlocks the first humble dash-cut version

Why it is good:
- this is the first moment the Arc Cutter starts feeling like the later stylish form
- gives the player a visible new verb without skipping to full combo-combat complexity

What it does not do:
- no huge hitbox
- no full armor delete
- no boss-melter behavior

## Blaster Starter

Starter name:
- `Dock Blaster`

### Base State

Starting weaknesses:

- narrow shot
- mediocre cadence
- low stagger
- no crowd utility

What the player learns:

- safer than Arc Cutter
- useful for scouting and suppression
- unsatisfying if used as the only answer

### Upgrade 1: `Refit Barrel`

Effect:
- slightly faster recovery and slightly cleaner shot speed

Why it is good:
- makes the gun feel less mushy
- does not meaningfully change power fantasy

What it does not do:
- no damage spike
- no extra projectile

### Upgrade 2: `Twin Feed`

Effect:
- every third shot adds a tiny second tracer

Why it is good:
- starts crowd utility
- helps suppress `Mite Drifter` pairs and `Brood Pod` spills

What it does not do:
- no shotgun spread
- no lane wipe

### Upgrade 3: `Target Coil`

Effect:
- the blaster gains a mild auto-correct / weak-point pull on valid targets

Why it is good:
- makes the weak gun feel more precise instead of merely faster
- improves suppression against `Mite Drifter` and pod spill without becoming a lock-on delete

What it does not do:
- no full armor bypass
- no high burst damage

### Upgrade 4: `Pressure Spike`

Effect:
- armored or pod targets build pressure faster and open earlier under sustained fire

Why it is good:
- clarifies the blaster's real job in the swap economy
- gives the player a satisfying setup tool before they get richer crowd control later

What it does not do:
- no giant raw DPS jump
- no crowd wipe
- no replacement for melee finish value

## Upgrade Delivery

The player should not get these from abstract menus first.

Use:

- ship workbench
- salvage parts
- one technician NPC later
- in-world upgrade moments tied to the first slice

For the first vertical slice:

- the first chosen-weapon upgrade should happen automatically after the first combat strip
- the player should see that upgrades are diegetic, not detached UI trees
- the unchosen weapon should return only after the first rival boss, not before

## Recommended First-Slice Path

### If player picks Arc Cutter

Give:
- `Sharpened Edge`

Why:
- best first quality-of-life fix
- immediately felt
- easiest to understand

### If player picks Blaster

Give:
- `Refit Barrel`

Why:
- best first fix for weak-gun frustration
- improves responsiveness without flattening the combat identity

## Mid-Game Comparison

The current live playable combat should feel like:

- Arc Cutter after `Sharpened Edge + Lift Notch + Rebound Spring + Dash Motor`
- Blaster after `Refit Barrel + Twin Feed + Target Coil + Pressure Spike`

That is why the opening needs to start weaker.

## Alternate Weapon Return

The player should not simply find the second weapon in a locker.

The better rule is:

- choose a weak starter early
- grow it through the first `Hive Shaft` mission set
- defeat a boss wielding the perfected version of the alternate weapon
- salvage the damaged starter-grade version afterward

Reference:
- [STARTER-RIVAL-BOSS.md](/C:/Users/mzwin/Downloads/videogame/STARTER-RIVAL-BOSS.md)

This preserves the starter fantasy while still opening the game into a real dual-weapon structure at the right time.

## Failure Modes

- If the weak starter feels annoying, players will bounce before the upgrade.
- If the first upgrade is too small, the system feels fake.
- If the first upgrade is too large, growth collapses.
- If both trees solve the same problems the same way, the choice is cosmetic.

## Approval Bar

Ship the early weapon progression only if:

- both starters feel intentionally underbuilt, not underdesigned
- first upgrade is noticeable in under one minute
- Arc Cutter and Blaster still create different player behavior
- the player can imagine a future build without already having it
