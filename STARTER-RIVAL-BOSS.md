# Starter Rival Boss

## Product Call

The first boss should be a `starter rival boss`.

This is the cleanest version of the Pokemon-style dynamic:

- the player picks a starter weapon early
- the unchosen weapon stays emotionally active
- the first boss uses the terrifying perfected version of that alternate weapon
- beating the boss gives the player the damaged starter-grade version of it

That makes the first boss more than a gate.
It becomes the moment the game reveals its real dual-weapon identity.

## Why It Works

- the starter choice matters for the whole opening and first `Hive Shaft` set
- the player sees what the other weapon can become at its peak
- the boss creates envy and aspiration, not just obstruction
- the reward timing is elegant: mastery first, unlock second

## Core Rule

The boss is not a fair mirror match.

It is a `superior preview`.

That means:

- same weapon family as the unchosen starter
- stronger movement package
- 2 to 3 signature moves the player cannot do yet
- visibly royal, overclocked, or sanctioned version
- the post-fight reward is the stripped-down field version, not the boss version

## Branch A: Player Chose Arc Cutter

Boss weapon:
- overclocked `Maximum Blaster`

Boss read:
- oppressive lane control
- smart tracking bursts
- better pod/mite suppression than the player has access to
- shell-pressure tools the player's future blaster can grow into later

Fight fantasy:
- the player survives superior ranged control with the riskier close-range weapon
- closing distance feels heroic
- the game proves the blaster is not the boring option

Reward:
- salvage the damaged `Dock Blaster`
- it starts weak and practical, not elite

## Branch B: Player Chose Blaster

Boss weapon:
- overclocked `Maximum Arc Cutter`

Boss read:
- fast gap closers
- stronger rebound chains
- sharper rising punishes
- better dash-cut flow than the player can do yet

Fight fantasy:
- the player manages spacing against a close-range weapon that looks terrifying and stylish
- the game proves the cutter is not just reckless flavor

Reward:
- salvage the damaged `Service Fang`
- it starts weak and underbuilt, not heroic

## Reward Timing

The player should earn the alternate weapon:

- after the first `Hive Shaft` mission trio
- after the rival boss
- through salvage or recovery in the boss aftermath

That is the right timing because:

- the opening choice matters long enough
- the player understands their starter's weakness first
- the game becomes truly dual-weapon before it feels one-note

## Reward Rule

Do not hand over the rival's full-power version.

The boss uses:
- `Maximum Blaster` or `Maximum Arc Cutter`

The player receives:
- `Dock Blaster` or `Service Fang`

That preserves progression and keeps the boss special.

## Visual Rule

The rival boss should look unmistakably superior.

Use:
- brighter royal accents
- more complete armor or weapon silhouette
- larger attack arcs or richer projectile patterns
- stronger anticipation and recovery poses

The salvaged player version should look:
- worn
- field-repaired
- incomplete
- clearly the same family, but not the same tier

## Production Rule

Do not build this boss as a giant bespoke endgame encounter.

For Slice 01, the boss only needs:

- one strong arena
- one weapon-family branch swap
- one elite movement package
- one clear unlock aftermath

That is enough to make the opening weapon choice feel real.
