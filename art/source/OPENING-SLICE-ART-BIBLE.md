# Opening Slice Art Bible

This document turns the existing visual doctrine into a production rulebook for the first 10 minutes of `Killer Queen`.

It sharpens `DESIGN.md`, `PIXEL-ART-FUNDAMENTALS.md`, `STAGE-01-VISUAL-BAR.md`, and `PLAYER_ANIMATION_BIBLE.md`.
It does not replace them.

## Visual Thesis

The opening slice should read as:

`Capcom 16-bit action readability in the play plane, with Sonic CD richness in the background and set dressing`

The player fantasy is not neutral sci-fi.
It is a fast royal hunter moving through a fallen insect kingdom that was industrialized, cracked open, and left to glow at dusk.

The first 10 minutes must sell:

- the hero is stylish immediately
- Fang and Blaster are both real primary weapons
- the world has authored drama, not placeholder rectangles
- the boss feels like a rival duel, not a bigger enemy

## SNES First, Sonic CD Second

Use this split every time a shot or sprite starts drifting:

- `SNES first` means collision edges, attack lanes, silhouettes, hit timing, and gameplay read win every argument
- `Sonic CD second` means richer skies, mood lighting, layered signage, scenic color rhythm, and stronger background atmosphere

Allowed Sonic CD influence:

- lush sky gradients behind the gameplay plane
- color-scripted background layers
- cleaner metallic shine on landmark objects
- stylized signage, rails, and decorative machinery
- strong setpiece transitions around the rival duel and route-out corridor

Disallowed Sonic CD influence:

- soft-focus or blurry pixel surfaces
- oily gradients on character sprites
- equal-value foreground and background detail
- decorative saturation that competes with hazards or the player
- glossy pseudo-HD treatment

## Camera And Readability Rules

- Assume the game is read at gameplay zoom, not in a sprite sheet viewer.
- The player is the first read on every normal screen.
- During combat, the nearest live threat is the second read.
- During traversal, the next safe landing or route cue is the second read.
- Backgrounds can be richer only after those reads are protected.
- Platform tops need the thickest and cleanest edge treatment in the room.
- Never let the hottest accent color sit on deep background props.

## Silhouette Doctrine

### Player

- Blade-led silhouette even when the current weapon is Blaster
- Stable anchors: helmet, torso block, boots, jet nozzles
- Compact gun module, never a giant rifle
- Clean forward lean in motion states

### Rival

- Reads as a real bounty hunter, not a palette-swapped evil clone
- Orange-armored tactical silhouette with harder shoulder and forearm blocking
- Opposite starter weapon is the visual focal cue after the head and chest
- Overclocked, not oversized

### Environment

- Each room gets one signature silhouette
- Armory: rails and ceremonial rack spacing
- Shaft: ribbed vertical constriction and broken service braces
- Cave foyer: threshold arch and held tension
- Boss arena: broad duel floor plus two to three support platforms
- Route-out corridor: clear forward vector, not a puzzle room

## Material Language

- Armor: clean hard clusters, selective hot highlights, no pillow shading
- Ruin stone: chipped masses, readable breaks, no noisy dithering wallpaper
- Hive material: ribbed, chitin-adjacent shapes with industrial seams
- Metal: use specular accents sparingly; save brightest hits for focal props
- FX cores: hottest read at contact, then collapse quickly

## Animation Bar

The opening slice should feel expensive in motion, but never mushy.

- Anticipation is short
- Contact is the clearest frame
- Recovery returns to control quickly
- Boss telegraphs must read before danger starts
- Salvage and route-clear beats stay in motion instead of freezing into a long reward screen

Hero movement-specific requirements:

- Fang attack variants should visibly redirect the player
- Blaster shots should visibly recoil the player and pop enemies
- Landing and recovery frames must look controlled, not floppy

## UI And Presentation Rules

- The world teaches first; UI frames second
- Keep the front-door overlay thin and ceremonial, not informationally dense
- The starter choice should read from world-space rack lighting, weapon silhouette, and prompt guide
- The boss bar should feel like one clear tactical strip, not a debug slab
- Typography should be sharp, arcade, and restrained

## Hard Do Not Do

- Do not imitate generic neon sci-fi or modern live-service UI
- Do not use random glow stacks, card soup, or excessive chrome
- Do not make the cave a flat black tunnel
- Do not make the rival read as a demon or corruption variant
- Do not hide collision in texture noise
- Do not smooth, airbrush, or upscale pixels into mush
- Do not spend the same accent color on player, hazard, and deep background in one shot

## Approval Gate

The opening slice is on-bar only if:

- screenshots read in one glance
- the player and rival each have a memorable silhouette
- rooms feel authored, not generated
- Sonic CD influence enriches the frame without weakening the lane reads
- Fang, Blaster, and the rival duel all look worth playing before the player touches a button
