# FX Prompt Pack

Source brief: `art/source/briefs/fx-opening-slice-brief.md`

Use these prompts with the current combat sheet notes and the opening-slice art bible.

## Asset Group: Fang Combat FX

### Concept

Design Fang effects as clean lane-shaping arcs for a fast SNES action-platformer.
The effect should explain movement direction and contact timing in one glance.
Warm outer edge, cool inner core.
The style is hand-authored and tactical, not magical or smeared.

### Production Sprite

Produce final pixel-art Fang FX for `combat_fx_sheet.aseprite`.
Cover neutral slash, rising slash, and dive slash.
Keep contact frames bright and short.
Match the attack lane rather than producing oversized crescents.
Ensure the player body remains readable behind or beside the effect.

### Repair/Review

Review the Fang FX for lane clarity, timing, and body preservation.
Check whether each slash direction is readable without animation notes.
Reduce width, brightness, or frame linger anywhere the FX dominates the hero or hides terrain.

## Asset Group: Blaster Combat FX

### Concept

Design Blaster effects as explosive, compact, and recoil-explanatory.
The muzzle burst should feel like a short brutal shotgun bloom.
Pellet impacts should pop enemies and surfaces with heavy force.
These effects need to explain why the player moved after the shot.

### Production Sprite

Produce final pixel-art Blaster FX for `combat_fx_sheet.aseprite` or `combat_fx_sheet_boss.aseprite` if needed.
Cover muzzle burst, recoil burst, pellet impact, and hit confirm support.
Use hot white only at the core, with brass-orange heat and ember fringe around it.
Decay quickly back to readability.

### Repair/Review

Review the Blaster FX for recoil readability, impact authority, and contrast discipline.
Check whether the muzzle and impact explain force without flooding the screen.
If the effect reads like a generic sci-fi blast, push it back toward compact industrial violence.

## Asset Group: Boss, Salvage, And Route FX

### Concept

Design boss telegraphs, vulnerable cues, salvage sparks, and route-clear cues for the cave rival sequence.
Telegraphs should feel tactical and mechanical.
Salvage should feel valuable and still in motion.
Route-clear should feel like a short premium release, not a huge reward overlay.

### Production Sprite

Produce final pixel-art boss sequence FX for the combat sheet extension.
Support telegraph warnings, weak-state vent cue, salvage trail, defeat breakup sparks, and route-clear cue.
Keep floor edges and boss silhouettes readable during all warning states.
Use warm gold and white for salvage, amber and ember for danger, and restrained cyan only for system-tech edges.

### Repair/Review

Review the boss sequence FX for warning clarity, reward energy, and screen discipline.
Check whether telegraphs are legible before danger begins.
Check whether salvage feels premium without pausing play.
Cut any effect that behaves like a screen wipe or hides the duel space.
