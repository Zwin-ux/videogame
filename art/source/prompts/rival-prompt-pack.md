# Rival Prompt Pack

Source brief: `art/source/briefs/rival-hunter-brief.md`

Use these prompts with `OPENING-SLICE-ART-BIBLE.md` and the cave boss sequence notes.

## Asset Group: Rival Base Body

### Concept

Design the first cave boss as a rival bounty hunter, not a monster and not an evil recolor of the hero.
The silhouette should read orange-armored tactical hunter with harder shoulders and forearms than the player.
The body should feel slightly bigger and more planted than the hero while still being fast.
This is a professional killer competing for the same claim, not a demon.

### Production Sprite

Produce the rival base body frames for `rival_hunter_sheet.aseprite`.
Support standoff, flourish, telegraph hold, vulnerable, hurt, defeat, and salvage eject beats.
Make the head, chest, and weapon side readable at gameplay zoom.
Use tactical orange and warm ivory with restraint.
Do not bloat the frame or drown it in villain clutter.

### Repair/Review

Review the rival base body for human readability, pose confidence, and boss-screen separation.
Check whether the rival still reads clearly against the cave wall and telegraph FX.
If the rival looks too close to the player, push posture and armor blocking before adding more detail.

## Asset Group: Overclocked Fang Variant

### Concept

Design the rival Fang variant as a heavier cutting specialist using the player's unchosen melee weapon in a more dangerous form.
The weapon should feel tuned for dash cuts and sky cleaves.
The read is overclocked and professional, not oversized and wild.
Make the stance communicate threat before motion begins.

### Production Sprite

Produce Fang-variant combat frames for `rival_hunter_sheet.aseprite`.
Support dash cut, sky cleave, telegraph hold, vulnerable recovery, and hurt.
Let the blade vents or edges flare only during real attack moments.
Preserve room for the player silhouette and hit effects during overlap.

### Repair/Review

Review the Fang variant for immediate weapon recognition and telegraph clarity.
Check that dash cut and sky cleave have distinct reads before impact.
If the weapon looks like a fantasy sword or blocks too much of the body, simplify and tighten it.

## Asset Group: Overclocked Blaster Variant

### Concept

Design the rival Blaster variant as a compact breacher running a more dangerous version of the player's unchosen shotgun weapon.
The read should sell lane burst and fan sweep control.
This weapon is disciplined and overpowered, not big for its own sake.
The recoil mastery should look intentional and intimidating.

### Production Sprite

Produce Blaster-variant combat frames for `rival_hunter_sheet.aseprite`.
Support lane burst, fan sweep, telegraph hold, vulnerable recovery, and hurt.
Use vent glow and muzzle heat only during active attacks.
Keep the weapon compact and readable.
Do not let it become a long heavy cannon.

### Repair/Review

Review the Blaster variant for recoil authority, threat read, and gameplay clarity.
Check whether the player can tell what attack family is coming before it fires.
Reduce decorative heat or muzzle clutter if it competes with the actual threat lane.
