# Stage Prompt Pack

Source brief: `art/source/briefs/stage-opening-slice-brief.md`

Use these prompts with `STAGE-01-VISUAL-BAR.md` and `OPENING-SLICE-PALETTE-STAGING-MATRIX.md`.

## Asset Group: Starter Armory

### Concept

Design the starter armory as a ceremonial but usable deck inside a live shipboard world.
The dominant read is the player and the active weapon rack.
The room should feel like a command-space threshold, not a storage closet and not a UI wall.
Use strong rails, deck striping, and clear rack silhouettes.
Keep the background quiet so world-space teaching can dominate.

### Production Sprite

Produce the armory tile and prop art for `stage_01_tiles.aseprite` and `stage_01_props.aseprite`.
Prioritize readable walkable surfaces, strong rack lighting states, and one memorable deck silhouette.
Use warm gold rack pulses sparingly and keep platform tops cleaner than decorative trim.
Avoid narrow visual clutter around the prompt zone.

### Repair/Review

Review the starter armory for first-glance clarity.
Check whether the active rack is obvious without footer text.
Check whether the room feels ceremonial and authored rather than flat.
Remove decorative clutter that competes with the player or weapon racks.

## Asset Group: First Shaft

### Concept

Design the first shaft as a combat-and-recovery climb, not one long anonymous vertical tunnel.
The room language should use ribbed constriction, broken service braces, and clear next-land vectors.
The player should feel pressure, recovery, and upward intent in the layout.
Background richness can suggest scale, but platforms and threats must stay cleaner.

### Production Sprite

Produce shaft tiles, braces, hazard seams, and supporting background layers for `stage_01_tiles.aseprite` and `stage_01_backgrounds.aseprite`.
Platform tops must read instantly.
Hazard seams use amber and ember sparingly.
Keep the breach light above visible as a route promise.
Do not texture-fill surfaces into mush.

### Repair/Review

Review the shaft art for traversal clarity and signature shape.
Check whether the player always knows the next safe landing.
Check whether the background is quieter than collision.
Simplify repeated forms until the shaft feels authored instead of procedural.

## Asset Group: Cave Foyer, Boss Arena, And Route-Out Corridor

### Concept

Design the cave foyer, boss arena, and route-out corridor as one authored dramatic sequence.
The cave foyer should feel like a held breath.
The boss arena should feel like a duel room with support platforms, not a cave wallpaper box.
The route-out corridor should decompress and point east toward the mountain line without becoming a whole new biome.
Use Sonic CD richness mostly in the route-out distance layer, not in the boss fight plane.

### Production Sprite

Produce cave threshold props, arena floor and wall sets, support platforms, weak-state support cues, and route-out corridor assets for the stage sheets.
Keep the boss arena simple enough that rival telegraphs and vulnerable states stay obvious.
Use quiet cave practicals, strong floor edges, and one clear chamber arch.
Let the route-out corridor open into dusk violet, rust rose, and gold distance color while keeping the path obvious.

### Repair/Review

Review the cave sequence for duel readability and emotional arc.
Check whether the foyer reads as tension, the arena reads as combat, and the route-out reads as release.
Cut any cave texture, background glow, or scenic prop that hurts the player-versus-boss silhouette battle.
If the exit line is ambiguous, redesign the route-out composition before adding more detail.
