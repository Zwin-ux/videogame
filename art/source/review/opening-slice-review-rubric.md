# Opening Slice Review Rubric

Score every asset or screen from 0 to 10 in each category.
Anything below 7 in a gameplay-facing category is a reject for the opening slice.

## Categories

### 1. Silhouette Clarity

- `10`: readable in one glance at gameplay zoom, memorable shape, no identity confusion
- `7`: mostly clear, minor crowding or over-detail
- `5`: readable only after a second look
- `3`: silhouette fights itself or blends into its backdrop
- `0`: unreadable in play

### 2. Gameplay Readability

- `10`: player, threats, floor edges, and interactables all resolve instantly
- `7`: one minor conflict, but play still reads
- `5`: active effects or detail regularly obscure the lane
- `3`: the player has to search for landings, prompts, or danger
- `0`: art actively misleads play

### 3. Palette Discipline

- `10`: accent colors are reserved, values are controlled, focal hierarchy is obvious
- `7`: mostly disciplined, one area slightly too loud
- `5`: saturation or contrast starts flattening hierarchy
- `3`: everything is equally bright or equally muted
- `0`: palette destroys legibility

### 4. Animation Clarity

- `10`: anticipation, contact, and recovery all read cleanly
- `7`: motion is good, but one beat is soft
- `5`: contact timing or direction is unclear
- `3`: frames feel mushy or floppy
- `0`: movement reads worse in motion than as a still

### 5. Room Hierarchy

- `10`: the eye order is controlled and intentional
- `7`: one secondary area competes, but the main read survives
- `5`: room contains multiple equal reads
- `3`: scenic dressing overwhelms navigation or combat
- `0`: room has no dominant focal point

### 6. Style Consistency

- `10`: clearly belongs to `Killer Queen`, matches the opening-slice doctrine, no outside drift
- `7`: one minor reference leak or material inconsistency
- `5`: feels like mixed inspiration instead of one game
- `3`: reads like a template, fan mockup, or unrelated project
- `0`: visually off-brand

## Gate Rules

- Player body, weapons, FX, prompts, hazards, and boss telegraphs must all hit at least `8` in gameplay readability.
- Front-door UI and boss arena must hit at least `8` in room hierarchy.
- Anything that scores `6` or lower in palette discipline must be simplified before extra detail is added.
- If silhouette clarity and gameplay readability disagree, gameplay readability wins and the asset is revised.

## Review Checklist

- Check the asset or screen at gameplay zoom first.
- Check it over a dark background and a bright background.
- Check it with one live enemy or boss silhouette present if relevant.
- Check it with one active effect if relevant.
- Check it again in motion if animated.
- Write the single biggest failure in one sentence before proposing fixes.

## Common Reject Reasons

- too much decorative noise in the play plane
- accent colors spent on everything
- menu panel looks like a debug box
- text wraps into stacked fragments
- telegraph reads after the attack, not before it
- player and boss blend into the cave wall
- route cue is weaker than background atmosphere
